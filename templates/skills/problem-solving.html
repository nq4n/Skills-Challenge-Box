Problem Solving

Define the issue, find the cause, try options, and deliver a working fix.

Take Quiz

About this skill

Problem solving is a structured way to move from a messy situation to a useful result. It combines clear thinking, creativity, testing, and communication.

  • Why it matters in jobs: Teams need people who can turn blockers into results without waiting for perfect instructions.
  • Daily practice: Write the problem in one sentence, list causes, try a small experiment, measure, adjust.
  • Avoid these mistakes: Jumping to solutions, ignoring data, solving the symptom not the cause, or never measuring the outcome.

The core steps

  1. Define: State the problem clearly. What is happening? Who is affected? What is the impact?
  2. Measure: Collect facts (numbers, examples, screenshots, logs). Set a success target.
  3. Analyze: Look for root causes (5 Whys, fishbone). Separate facts from guesses.
  4. Ideate: Generate multiple options. Consider trade-offs: cost, time, risk, quality.
  5. Decide & Test: Choose a promising option. Run a small, low-risk pilot or prototype.
  6. Implement: Apply the solution fully with a simple plan, roles, and timeline.
  7. Review: Did it hit the target? Capture lessons and next steps.

  • Support: Ticket backlog grows on Mondays ‚Üí add weekend FAQ + auto-triage ‚Üí 35% fewer Monday tickets.
  • Education: Students miss deadlines ‚Üí introduce weekly roadmap & reminders ‚Üí on-time submissions rise.

Practice now (10 minutes)

  1. Pick a small annoyance you had this week.
  2. Write a one-sentence problem statement and a success target.
  3. Do a quick 5 Whys to find a likely root cause.
  4. List 3 solutions, pick one, and run a mini experiment today.
  5. Record the result and one lesson learned.

Signals employers look for

  • Clear problem statements and measurable goals.
  • Comfort with data and quick experiments.
  • Explaining trade-offs and reasoning, not just the final answer.
  • Closing the loop: documenting outcomes and preventing repeat issues.

Common pitfalls & how to fix them

  • Solution first, facts later: Pause and gather evidence before acting.
  • Over-engineering: Prefer the simplest option that meets the goal.
  • No owner: Assign a clear DRI (directly responsible individual) and deadline.
  • Not measuring: Define success metrics up front and check them after rollout.
